Our story

A fair wage.
Woven into everything we make.

Every piece starts in the same place — a handloom shed in Tamil Nadu, run by someone who's been weaving for years.

We don't own a factory. We work with weaving co-ops and tailoring units that already existed, that have been making this cloth for generations, in the same districts their families have always worked in.

What we changed is who gets paid first and how much. We pay the co-op on completion, at a rate we agree on before the order is placed, not whatever the market has gotten used to paying. No auction house, no export agent taking a cut in the middle.

That's the whole model. Source from the people who already do this work, pay them like the work matters, and let the cloth speak for the rest.

"We're not trying to save a tradition. We're trying to pay it what it's owed."

How a piece gets made

  1. Sourcing the thread

    Cotton and silk bought direct from regional growers, at a fixed price agreed before the season starts.

  2. Handwoven on the loom

    Set and woven by hand, at the pace and pattern the weaver chooses, not a production line.

  3. Cut and stitched locally

    Finished by tailoring units in the same district, people who've done this work for years.

  4. Shipped from the source

    Your order leaves the co-op that made it. We don't warehouse or sit on stock.

Paid on completion, not on consignment after the sale.

Every order traceable back to the loom that made it.

No workshop in our supply chain that we haven't visited ourselves.
